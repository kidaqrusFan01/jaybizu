Safe Drinking Water for 3 Villages in Ghana

CW
by CleanWater Initiative
✓ Verified nonprofit
💧
About
Updates (3)
Donors (284)

Over 1.2 million people in rural Ghana lack access to clean drinking water. Contaminated sources cause preventable deaths, keep children out of school, and trap communities in cycles of poverty.

This project will drill two boreholes and install modern filtration systems across three villages — Kpetoe, Asikuma, and Dome — giving 3,400 people safe water for generations. Your donation funds drilling equipment, trained local engineers, and a 5-year maintenance program run by elected community water committees.

Project milestones
  • ✓
    Site surveys completed — Geological surveys confirmed borehole locations in all 3 villages.
  • ✓
    Community water committees formed — 15 elected members trained in management and maintenance.
  • ✓
    Borehole drilling — Kpetoe village — First borehole operational, serving 1,100 people.
  • ○
    Borehole drilling — Asikuma village — Scheduled to begin once 70% funding is reached.
  • ○
    Borehole drilling — Dome village — Final installation, pending full funding.
  • ○
    Filtration systems + handwashing stations — Full hygiene infrastructure for all sites.
May 14, 2024
🎉 Kpetoe borehole is now operational!
The first borehole in Kpetoe village has been completed and is fully operational. Over 1,100 residents now have access to clean, safe drinking water — many for the first time in their lives.
March 28, 2024
Drilling equipment arrives on site
Our drilling rig and equipment arrived safely in Kpetoe after a 3-day journey. The local engineering crew is assembled and drilling begins Monday.
February 10, 2024
Water committees elected and trained
Community members in all three villages elected their water management committees. 15 committee members learned basic maintenance, record-keeping, and how to manage long-term upkeep.

    Our mission

    CleanWater Initiative exists to end the water crisis — one borehole, one filtration system, one community at a time. Founded in 2011 by engineers and public health professionals, we've delivered clean water to over 128,000 people across Sub-Saharan Africa and Southeast Asia.

    We don't just build infrastructure — we build lasting capacity. Every project includes elected community water committees, maintenance training, and a 5-year support program to ensure nothing breaks down.
