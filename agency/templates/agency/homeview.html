Safe Drinking Water for 3 Villages in Ghana

CW
by CleanWater Initiative
✓ Verified
💧
About
Updates (3)
Donors (284)

Over 1.2 million people in rural Ghana lack access to clean drinking water. Contaminated sources cause preventable deaths, keep children out of school, and trap communities in cycles of poverty.

This project will drill two boreholes and install modern filtration systems across three villages — giving 3,400 people safe water for generations.

    Our mission

    CleanWater Initiative exists to end the water crisis — one borehole, one filtration system, one community at a time. Founded in 2011 by engineers and public health professionals, we've delivered clean water to over 128,000 people across Sub-Saharan Africa and Southeast Asia.

    Every project includes elected community water committees, maintenance training, and a 5-year support program to ensure lasting results.
